# SaaS Marketing Patterns

Specific guidance for software-as-a-service marketing sites.

## SaaS-Specific Challenges

- **Intangible product**: Can't touch or see it before buying
- **Ongoing relationship**: Subscription = continuous value delivery
- **Complex decisions**: Often involves multiple stakeholders
- **High competition**: Many alternatives, easy switching

## Trial & Onboarding

### Free Trial Best Practices

**Trial length:**
- Simple products: 7 days
- Complex products: 14-30 days
- Goal: Enough time to see value, not so long they forget

**Trial structure:**
- No credit card upfront (higher conversion to trial)
- Credit card required (higher conversion to paid, lower volume)

**Onboarding during trial:**
1. Welcome email with quick start
2. In-app checklist to key actions
3. Day 3: Check-in email
4. Day 7: Value reminder
5. Day 10: Trial ending soon
6. Day 14: Last chance + offer

### Freemium vs Free Trial

| Freemium | Free Trial |
|----------|------------|
| Free forever, limited | Full product, limited time |
| Good for: virality, self-serve | Good for: complex products |
| Risk: users never upgrade | Risk: users don't activate |
| Example: Slack, Notion | Example: Salesforce |

## SaaS-Specific Copy Tips

### Value Metrics
Frame around user outcomes:
- ❌ "Powerful project management"
- ✅ "Ship projects 40% faster"

### Time-to-Value
Emphasize quick setup:
- "Start in 2 minutes"
- "See results in week one"
- "No implementation project"

### Risk Reversal
Remove friction:
- "No credit card required"
- "Cancel anytime"
- "Free migration from [Competitor]"
- "30-day money-back guarantee"
