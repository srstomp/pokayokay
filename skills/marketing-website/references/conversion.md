### Testimonials That Convert

**High-Impact Structure:**
```
"[Specific result/outcome achieved]"

[Photo] [Full Name]
        [Title], [Company]
```

**What Makes Testimonials Believable:**
- **Specificity**: Numbers, timeframes, before/after
- **Identity**: Real name, real photo, real company
- **Relevance**: From someone like your target customer
- **Transformation**: What changed for them

**Weak vs Strong:**
| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Great product!" | "We reduced churn by 34% in 3 months" |
| "Easy to use" | "My whole team was onboarded in an afternoon" |
| "Highly recommend" | "We switched from Competitor and saved $2k/month" |
| Anonymous | Sarah Chen, VP Engineering at TechCorp |

### Trust Badges

**Types:**
- Security: SSL, SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA
- Reviews: G2, Capterra, TrustPilot ratings
- Press: "Featured in TechCrunch"
- Guarantees: "30-day money back"
- Payment: Visa, Mastercard, PayPal logos

**Placement:**
- Near pricing/payment
- In footer
- On checkout page
- Near forms asking for sensitive info

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

Show higher price first to make others seem reasonable:
```
Enterprise $299 â†’ Pro $79 â†’ Starter $19
```

The $79 looks reasonable compared to $299.

### Decoy Effect

Add a "decoy" option that makes target option look better:
```
Basic $10 (5 features)
Plus  $25 (10 features)  â† target
Pro   $27 (11 features)  â† decoy makes Plus look like better value
```

### Price Presentation

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| $49.00 | $49 |
| $49/month | $49/mo |
| $588/year | $49/mo (billed annually) |
| Starting at $49 | From $49/mo |

### Reducing Price Friction

**Reframe the cost:**
- "Less than a coffee a day"
- "Pays for itself in one saved hour"
- "$49/mo vs $200+/hr consultant"

**Risk reversal:**
- "30-day money-back guarantee"
- "Cancel anytime"
- "No credit card required to start"
- "Free plan forever"

### Pricing Page Elements

1. **Clear plan names**: Starter, Pro, Team (not Gold, Platinum)
2. **Feature comparison table**: What's in each plan
3. **Recommended highlight**: "Most Popular" badge
4. **Annual discount**: Show savings clearly
5. **FAQ**: Address pricing objections
6. **Enterprise CTA**: "Contact sales" for custom needs
7. **Guarantee**: Risk reversal statement

## Objection Handling

### Common Objections & Responses

**"Too expensive"**
- Show ROI calculator
- Compare to cost of problem
- Compare to alternatives (competitor or manual process)
- Offer payment plans
- Highlight money-back guarantee

**"I need to think about it"**
- Remind of pain point they mentioned
- Share relevant case study
- Offer limited-time bonus
- Ask what's holding them back

**"I need to check with my team"**
- Provide shareable summary
- Offer team demo
- Give comparison assets
- ROI documentation for stakeholders

**"What if it doesn't work?"**
- Money-back guarantee
- Free trial period
- Customer success support
- Case studies from similar companies

**"I've tried something like this before"**
- Acknowledge past experience
- Differentiate your approach
- Specific proof points
- Lower-commitment trial

### Objection Placement

Address objections proactively:
- In FAQ section
- Near pricing
- In testimonials (let customers address them)
- In comparison tables

## A/B Testing Priorities

### Highest Impact Tests

1. **Headlines**: Often 20-50% conversion impact
2. **CTAs**: Text, color, placement
3. **Hero section**: Layout, imagery, copy
4. **Pricing presentation**: Plan names, feature display
5. **Social proof**: Testimonial selection, placement

### Testing Framework

1. **Hypothesis**: "Changing X will improve Y because Z"
2. **Single variable**: Test one thing at a time
3. **Statistical significance**: Wait for enough data
4. **Document learnings**: Build knowledge base

### Quick Wins to Test

- CTA button color (high contrast usually wins)
- CTA text (first person vs second person)
- Headline specificity (add numbers)
- Form field count (fewer usually wins)
- Social proof placement (closer to CTA)
